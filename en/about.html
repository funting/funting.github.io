About

Ting Huang is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Foreign Languages at National Chiayi University, Taiwan. She received her Ph.D. in Linguistics from National Tsing Hua University (2016), where her dissertation, Topics in Malaysian Mandarin Phonetics and Phonology, received both the Linguistic Society of Taiwan (LST) Best Dissertation Award and the Overseas Community Affairs Council (OCAC) Best Dissertation Award. She holds an M.A. in Linguistics from National Chengchi University and a B.A. in English, also from National Chengchi University.

She has held postdoctoral positions in the Department of Linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and at National Tsing Hua University, and worked as a Linguistics Specialist at ObEN Inc. She is currently an Affiliated Scientist at Haskins Laboratories. Her research centers on phonology, phonetics, and the phonetics–phonology and phonology–morphology interfaces, with a particular focus on Chinese linguistics, tone and prosody, articulatory phonology, coarticulation, and Austronesian phonetics.
